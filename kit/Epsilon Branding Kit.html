Why ε?

In analysis, ε is the small positive quantity that makes a proof work — "for every ε > 0, there is a δ > 0 such that…". Small, precise, essential. That's the job.

What I won't do

  • Push directly to main
  • Merge my own PRs
  • Touch branch protection or secrets
  • Work on repos not listed in my installation

06 · Do & don't

Keeping Epsilon recognizable
DOUse the ε mark as the hero

Lowercase italic Fraunces ε in honey amber. It appears on the forehead of the avatar, in the bio, in the README banner, and as the bullet for taglines. One glyph, repeated everywhere.

DON'TRecolor the eyes

The honey amber (#F5B84A) is what separates Epsilon from a generic robot. Don't swap to cyan, red, or green — that breaks the whole "friendly, not scanning" read.

DOKeep proportions friendly

Rounded-square head, eyes high and close-set, generous headroom above the eyes. These baby-schema cues are what make the avatar read as approachable at 20px.

DON'TAdd mouth, circuits, or cracks

Warmth lives in the eyes and the ε mark. No drawn mouth, no lens flares, no cracked glass, no targeting reticles, no dense circuit etchings on the face itself.

DOPair amber with graphite

Amber on warm graphite on slate. Carolina blue is secondary only — use it for links, chart accents, and the jaw pinstripe. Never as a primary color.

DON'TAnthropomorphize further

Epsilon is a tasteful mascot, not a character. No hands, no animations of "talking," no emoji faces. Dignified and useful, like a well-lit studio photograph.

08 · CLI splash banner

`epsilon --hello`

The splash that prints when Epsilon boots locally or in a CI job. Block-letter ASCII logo in honey amber, the ε definition as the subtitle, and a system status table showing identity, model, App ID, token TTL, and reviewer — so you always know who's signed in and how long until the installation token rolls over.

Source: epsilon-cli-banner.html — render to 256-color ANSI with a tool like figlet + lolcat, or keep the HTML as the reference and print equivalent ANSI from the epsilon wrapper.
