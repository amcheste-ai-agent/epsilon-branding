# Epsilon — Usage rules

Keep Epsilon recognizable. The avatar reads "friendly, thoughtful coding
agent" at 20 pixels — that legibility is the whole design, and small
deviations break it quickly. Do and don't below.

## Do

### Use the ε mark as the hero

Lowercase italic Fraunces `ε` in honey amber. It appears on the forehead
of the avatar, in the bio, in the README banner, and as the bullet for
taglines. One glyph, repeated everywhere.

### Keep proportions friendly

Rounded-square head, eyes high and close-set, generous headroom above
the eyes. These baby-schema cues are what make the avatar read as
approachable at 20 px.

### Pair amber with graphite

Amber on warm graphite on slate. Carolina blue is secondary only — use
it for links, chart accents, and the jaw pinstripe. Never as a primary
color.

## Don't

### Recolor the eyes

The honey amber (`#F5B84A`) is what separates Epsilon from a generic
robot. Don't swap to cyan, red, or green — that breaks the whole
"friendly, not scanning" read.

### Add mouth, circuits, or cracks

Warmth lives in the eyes and the ε mark. No drawn mouth, no lens flares,
no cracked glass, no targeting reticles, no dense circuit etchings on
the face itself.

### Anthropomorphize further

Epsilon is a tasteful mascot, not a character. No hands, no animations
of "talking", no emoji faces. Dignified and useful, like a well-lit
studio photograph.
